猫ねこの ゆき
neko no yuki
Yuki the Cat

これは 猫ねこ です。

kore wa neko desu.

This is a cat.

猫ねこの 名前なまえは ゆき です。

neko no namae wa yuki desu.

The cat's name is Yuki.

ゆきは 魚さかなが 好すき です。

yuki wa sakana ga suki desu.

Yuki likes fish.

今日きょう、ゆきは 部屋へやで 寝ねました。

kyou, yuki wa heya de nemashita.

Today, Yuki slept in the room.

とても 可愛かわいかった です。

totemo kawaikatta desu.

She was very cute.

Grammar Notes

Line 1: これは 猫 です。

This introduces the most basic Japanese sentence structure: A は B です (A is B).

• は (wa): This is the Topic Particle. Even though it's written with the hiragana 'ha', it is pronounced 'wa' when used as a particle. It tells the listener, "As for [Topic]...". Here, the topic is kore (this).
• です (desu): This works like "is/am/are" in English, placed at the very end of the sentence to make it polite.

Line 2: 猫の 名前は ゆき です。

• の (no): This is the Possessive Particle. It works exactly like an apostrophe-S ('s) in English. Neko (cat) + no + namae (name) = Cat's name. It links two nouns together.

Line 3: ゆきは 魚が 好き です。

• が (ga): This is the Subject Particle. When saying you "like" something in Japanese, you don't use the direct object particle. Instead, the thing you like is marked with ga.

• 好き (suki): This translates to "like", but grammatically in Japanese, it's actually an adjective (specifically a na-adjective). Literally, the sentence is closer to "As for Yuki, fish is likable."

Line 4: 今日、ゆきは 部屋で 寝ました。

• で (de): This is the Location of Action Particle. It marks the place where an event or action happens. Where did the sleeping happen? In the room (heya). So, it becomes heya de.

• 寝ました (nemashita): This is the past tense of the verb neru (to sleep). The -mashita ending makes it past tense and polite.

Line 5: とても 可愛かった です。

• とても (totemo): An adverb meaning "very".

• 可愛かった (kawaikatta): This is the past tense of the adjective kawaii (cute). In Japanese, "i-adjectives" conjugate (change shape) just like verbs do to show time! Dropping the final 'i' and adding 'katta' turns "is cute" into "was cute."
